The Cause

Every Lot Gives Back.

Every dollar raised on October 22 flows to the enduring missions of St. John's Abbey & St. John's University — fifteen decades of prayer, education and art on the shores of Lake Sagatagan.

St. John's Abbey

Founded in 1856, Saint John's Abbey is one of the largest Benedictine monasteries in the Western Hemisphere. Its monks have spent more than a century and a half building a community where work, prayer and hospitality are inseparable — and where art has always been part of the vocation, from Marcel Breuer's landmark abbey church to the hand-illuminated pages of The Saint John's Bible.

Proceeds support the care of the monastic community and the abbey's ministries of education, hospitality and the arts.

St. John's University

Set in 2,700 acres of Minnesota woods and lakes, Saint John's University has formed generations of students in the Benedictine tradition of serious study and common life. Its partnership with the College of Saint Benedict makes it one of the nation's most distinctive liberal-arts communities.

Proceeds support student scholarships and the university's programs in the fine arts — the same programs that shaped Father Tupa's own double vocation of monk and painter.
